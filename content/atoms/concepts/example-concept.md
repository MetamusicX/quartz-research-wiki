## Working definition

*Situated memory* is, in this fictional example, the proposition that remembering is bound to the specific site in which it occurs — not as metaphor but as constitutive condition. The concept is taken from [[../works/example-work|The Geography of Recall]] and elaborated by [[../people/example-person|Iris Holm]].

In a real atom, this section would carry a careful, group-specific definition — including disagreements with neighbouring uses of the term.
